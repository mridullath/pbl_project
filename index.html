Sign Sense

"Sign Language Interpreter for Inclusive Communication for All !!"

Problem Statement

Suppose, Mia is a traveller who needs to ask for directions but is unable to because she is not able speak due to her being speech impaired making her use a completely different language than any of us without that impairment. There is no simple, universal way for speech-impaired individuals to communicate their needs (like asking directions) to hearing people in real-world situations.

Literary Review

AAC devices support basic communication for people with speech impairments and are widely used in clinical settings, but they require training and are not suited for spontaneous interactions. Likewise, apps such as Google Live Transcribe and Talkitt are portable and easy to use, yet they are slow and not optimized for real-time, natural communication in everyday situations.

Innovation

Existing tools for speech-impaired communication mainly support basic needs and struggle with real-time interaction due to model limitations and training specificity. Additionally, sign language varies across regions, creating further communication barriers. Our solution addresses these issues by offering broader situational support and a language-selection feature, enabling more inclusive and effective communication in everyday scenarios.

Model

We built the frontend using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, with SQL for the translator database. A CNN model is trained and embedded into a Python backend using tools like MediaPipe and OpenCV for image translation. We also plan to add multilingual output using Google’s translation library. Finally, everything is integrated and deployed using Django.

Expected Results

Our solution is expected to achieve a significant improvement in communication efficiency and user satisfaction compared to existing tools.
